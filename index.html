What is the net working capital?
the net working capital is the difference a firm's current assets and current liabilities, it measures the company's liquidity and it's ability to meet short term obligations.
How to calculate net working capital?
To calculate net working capital you should use the following formula:
net working capital = current assets - current liabilities
example: A company has $5,460,000 in current assets and $1,604,000 worth of current liabilities, cualculate the net working capital.
using the net working capital formula the answer will be:
net working capital = current assets - current liabilities
net working capital = $5,460,000 - $1,604,000
net working capital = $3,856,000
Why use net working capital?
net working capital is usually used to measure the ability of a firm to meet it's short term obligations and it's also used to fund operations of the business.
